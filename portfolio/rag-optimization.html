Robust Optimization for RAG Retrieval

From heuristic similarity search to optimization-driven document selection.

RAG Optimization Mixed-Integer Programming LLM Evaluation High-Dimensional Embeddings Julia

Overview

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems typically retrieve the top-k (most similar) documents to a query embedding. The approach is simple and effective, but sensitive to embedding noise and prone to redundant or inconsistent sets. In this project, I reframed retrieval as an optimization problem, enabling explicit control over the trade-off between relevance, diversity, and robustness. By introducing controlled uncertainty into document embeddings, I developed a selection method that prioritizes documents whose relevance holds up even under worst-case perturbations.

Experiments on a Wikipedia-style QA dataset showed that the optimized framework achieves answer accuracy comparable to standard top-k retrieval while producing more consistent and interpretable retrieval behavior.

Key insight: Improving RAG is not just about maximizing similarity scores, but about selecting document sets that remain reliable under variation and noise.
